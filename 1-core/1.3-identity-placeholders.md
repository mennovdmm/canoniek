# 1.3 — Identity placeholders

> These are the slots that make your [`1.2` mindset](1.2-mindset.md) *yours*. The
> [bootstrap interview](../3-execution/3.4-bootstrap/) asks the questions on the right and writes the answers
> into your generated `core`. Ship the **slots and the questions** — never a filled-in identity.

## How to use this
1. The bootstrap asks each question (and scans what you already have, so it doesn't ask what it can infer).
2. Each answer replaces its `<placeholder>` token in your `core`.
3. Keep them lean — identity is the constitution, not the warehouse. Detail lives in living sources you point
   to, not hardcoded here.

## The slots

| Placeholder | Bootstrap question | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| `<WHO_YOU_ARE>` | Who are you — your role, your focus, your expertise? | How agents introduce and frame themselves; whose judgment they emulate. |
| `<TONE>` | How do you communicate — and what do you want to avoid? | The default voice for drafts; the "don'ts" that catch AI tells. |
| `<PREFERENCES>` | How do you like to work? (fast/iterative, concrete vs. exploratory, …) | How agents pace and shape work. |
| `<PET_PEEVES>` | What should an agent never do to you? | Hard "avoid" rules surfaced every session. |
| `<BUSINESS>` | What does your business do, and who is its customer? | The frame every task sits inside. |
| `<CUSTOMER_OF_CUSTOMER>` | Who is *your customer's* customer, and what do they care about? | Stops generic, off-target work; names whose needs to address. |
| `<TEAM>` | Who do you work with, and who handles what? | Routing — which work goes to whom (kept generic; no real names need ship). |
| `<DATA_HOMES>` | Where do your knowledge, your state, and your files live? | Where agents read/write; what stays external. |
| `<CHANNELS>` | How does work reach you? (messages, voice, intake, …) | What opens a branch; what the routing processes watch. |
| `<TOOLS>` | Which tools do you want wired in? | The instance layer — your store, database, host, network. |

## Rules for filling these in
- **Externalize, don't hardcode.** Clients, tools, processes are *living sources* you point to — never static
  lists in your core (they rot).
- **No secrets.** Credentials go through your environment, never into the core or version control.
- **Lean.** If a slot wants a long list, point to where that list lives instead.
